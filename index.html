Humans need to learn higher forms of love through experiencing lust and desire.

The prevalent form of love in contemporary human society is rooted in physical attraction, lust, and desire which primarily aims at self-satisfaction and stems from our animalistic nature.

Such relationships are often accompanied by pain and distress.

However, lust should not be dismissed‚Äîit has its purpose.

"Humans evolve from desire-based relationships to higher level connections, merging the mind, spirit, and soul."

Humans evolve from desire-based relationships to higher level connections, merging the mind, spirit, and soul. This journey of learning through desire is a critical lesson.

A meaningful relationship may begin with physical attraction but can grow into a higher-dimensional bond:

The Evolution of Love

Supporting each other during life's challenges. Seeing and becoming the best version of oneself. Nourishing the soul and enabling personal growth.

This applies to all relationships.

For relationships to be long-lasting, they must elevate in their energy.

Remaining solely in the realm of lustful love is unstable. Even if the relationship doesn't end, it lacks vitality internally.

At an even higher level, a relationship is no longer just for the benefit of two individuals. It becomes a partnership aimed at creating something greater and benefiting others.

Networking Up Begins with Self-Improvement

üìÖ January 2025 üìñ 4 min read üëÅ 8K+ readers

1. The people in your life resonate at a frequency similar to your own.

You naturally attract, and are attracted to, those who share your vibration. If you find yourself dissatisfied with the people around you, the first step is to look inward and change yourself.

While this is often called "networking up," it's really about self-improvement. As you better yourself, your social circle will naturally elevate with you.

"As you better yourself, your social circle will naturally elevate with you."

2. Seize the opportunity to make genuine friends while you're young.

This is why it's so important to make genuine friends while you're young. This isn't to say professional networking is unimportant; later in life, networking for resource exchange becomes inevitable.

At that stage, connections are often built on the social value you provide. It becomes a transactional exchange, and the opportunity to make true friends diminishes.

3. On a deeper level, the people around you are mirrors reflecting parts of yourself.

You'll notice that your friends often share your traits. If a quality in someone strongly attracts you, it's likely a quality you either possess or wish you had.

Conversely, if you strongly dislike a trait in others, you may dislike that same trait in yourself. Gaining this level of self-awareness requires introspection and isn't always obvious.

4. If socializing is something you enjoy, then fully embrace it.

Ultimately, the key is to be authentic. If you enjoy socializing, embrace it. If you prefer solitude, follow your heart.

When you focus on what you love and excel at it, people who resonate with your frequency will naturally gravitate toward you.

The Judging Mind: The Root of Inequality

üìÖ January 2025 üìñ 6 min read üëÅ 12K+ readers

At their source, all things in the universe are equal. They are without inherent distinction‚Äîno high or low, no superior or inferior.

Equality does not mean that everything is a carbon copy. It means seeing the world without a judging mind, without creating arbitrary divisions.

For example, the existence of different skin tones is an objective reality. But the human-made standards that label one as "inferior" are subjective. The universe itself makes no such judgments.

"As long as this judging mind exists in people, inequality will persist."

As long as this judging mind exists in people, inequality will persist.

Even if we were to achieve perfect economic equality, the judging mind would remain. It would simply find other traits, like physical appearance, to create new hierarchies of "superior" and "inferior."

We usually try to fix inequality on the material level. But the judging mind is not a material problem; it exists on the plane of spirit and consciousness.

Addressing this will be the great challenge of the coming age. We can only await its unfolding.

Life is ultimately a solo journey

üìÖ January 2025 üìñ 5 min read üëÅ 9K+ readers

1. Living your own life well is the prerequisite for any healthy relationship.

A good relationship means two people inspiring each other to become better. Only when you're in an uplifted state can you attract kindred spirits.

When you're in a low state, you're more likely to attract toxic relationships. They're often clouded by possessiveness, obsession, and jealousy. These low-energy connections usually bring pain.

When your soul is truly fulfilled, you'll realize that a good partner is a companion for mutual growth, not someone you cling to because you can't stand being alone.

A fulfilling relationship is built by two grounded, emotionally stable individuals who support and empower each other to become the best version of themselves.

But a toxic relationship often traps both in a cycle of taking. You know others can't give you what you need, yet you stay stuck because you're scared to walk away.

When you're inwardly adrift, lacking love, attention, or validation, you grasp outward‚Äîhoping someone else will give you steady, unchanging love.

2. What you seek is already within you.

When you're inwardly adrift, lacking love, attention, or validation, you grasp outward‚Äîhoping someone else will give you steady, unchanging love. You grow anxious over every reaction, every gesture. This may trap you in a toxic relationship, unable to leave.

Looking outside for salvation, but everything is impermanent. Even if you grasp it briefly, you'll handle it with anxious care, trading away pieces of yourself just to hold on.

3. No one will live your life for you. You hold the reins of your own existence.

Don't wait for a savior to pull you from life's mud or emotional quicksand. The only one who can do this is yourself. You don't have to be perfect. It's okay to stumble sometimes. True, quality companions often appear when you're living earnestly, boldly being yourself and striving upward.

4. No matter who you live with or how close you are, life is ultimately lived alone.

Life, at its core, is a solo journey. No one can live it for you. Treasure the connections that cross your path. Then, carrying all the lessons, growth, love, and courage, begin the next journey.

The 6 Stages of Conflict Resolution

üìÖ January 2025 üìñ 6 min read üëÅ 7K+ readers

1. Stage One: Self-Focused Reaction

Only seeing your own perspective. Emotional. Feeling misunderstood, angry: "Why are they treating me this way?"

2. Stage Two: Initial Reflection

Starting to examine your own role. Able to consider the other person's viewpoint: "Could I have handled this better?" "Their reaction might have valid reasons."

3. Stage Three: Mature Understanding (Emotional Maturity)

Able to comprehend and accept others' limitations, seeing their struggles and constraints.

With an open heart, you tolerate their behavior. Resolve conflict calmly.

Like elders dealing with youth: Having experienced that stage themselves, they understand without overreacting.

Those who practice "stepping back for a broader view", and resolve conflict peacefully, demonstrate genuine acceptance of others.

Accepting others helps you accept yourself: You reflect on your mistakes, but also acknowledge your own efforts.

You admit you sometimes err, acknowledge your faults, then improve and grow. Reaching this stage is true maturity. (This has nothing to do with age.)

4. Stage Four: Psychological Awareness (Where Modern Psychology Stops)

Recognizing all behavior has underlying triggers. Hurtful actions often stem from past wounds:

Example: People who constantly seek validation often lacked recognition earlier in life.

Example: Those deprived of childhood love may cling to unhealthy relationships, terrified of abandonment.

"What you lack internally, you seek externally."

5. Stage Five: Seeing Interconnected Causes

Understand that events spring from interwoven causality and synchronicity, where all conditions merge

Life's surface struggles feel like a temporary game‚Äî no need to obsess over gains/losses.

Every experience helps your growth. Trust: "This is happening for my development."

6. Stage Six: Unshakable Peace

Facing all events with undisturbed calm. Nothing disrupts your inner stillness.

Observing without labeling things "good/bad" or "right/wrong."

Watch life unfold like flowing water. Navigate worldly challenges, witness joys and sorrows‚Äî while abiding in deep peace and clarity.

"In an age where machines think for us, we're finally forced to discover what makes us truly human."

The Beginning

Like many people, I spent my teens overwhelmed by anxiety and endless overthinking. Life felt like an impossible puzzle with no solution. Everything changed when I discovered meditation and Eastern philosophy through a mentor who saw my struggle. What began as a desperate search for relief became the foundation of a completely different way of being.

The Transformation

Through consistent practice and deep self-inquiry, I learned that lasting change happens from within. The anxiety that once consumed me gradually gave way to inner calm. The mental chaos transformed into clarity. This journey revealed a fundamental truth: we all have the capacity for profound transformation‚Äîwe just need the right guidance and commitment to the process.

Why I Write

I write not from a place of superiority, but from deep recognition. I see my former self in every anxious mind, every restless heart seeking peace. After years of inner work and outer success, I realized that fulfillment brings responsibility. When you find your way out of pain, you naturally want to help others find theirs. The truth is simple: people in pain often cause pain. Those lacking love struggle to give love. Only when you fill your own cup can you pour into others. Our relentless pursuit of external material success has left our hearts empty. What is the meaning of life? What is the secret of happiness? Where do these philosophical and spiritual pursuits lead us? These are the questions of our era. We could have avoided them once, but in the age of AI, we have no choice but to confront them.
